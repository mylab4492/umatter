Frequently Asked Questions

Here are list of F.A.Q. please read before asking any questions.

  • Is coaching and counseling same?

    Coaching and counseling are distinct practices with different focuses and methods. Coaching helps individuals set and achieve personal or professional goals, emphasizing future-oriented actions and performance improvement. In contrast, counseling addresses psychological and emotional issues, often dealing with past experiences to foster healing and personal growth. While both require strong listening skills and provide supportive environments, coaching is more about goal-setting and accountability, whereas counseling delves into mental health and emotional well-being.

  • Does coach provide mental health service?

    Generally, coaches do not provide mental health services. Their primary role is to help clients set and achieve personal or professional goals, improve performance, and unlock potential. Coaching focuses on future-oriented actions and personal development rather than addressing psychological or emotional issues.

    However, some coaches may have training in areas that overlap with mental health, such as stress management or mindfulness, and can provide support in these areas. Itâs important to note that coaches are not typically licensed mental health professionals, and they should not diagnose or treat mental health conditions. If a client needs help with psychological or emotional issues, they should seek assistance from a licensed counselor, therapist, or other qualified mental health professional.

  • How can we measure the change?

    In coaching, measuring change involves a combination of quantitative and qualitative methods to track progress and demonstrate improvement. Initially, specific, measurable goals are set using criteria like SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound). Progress towards these goals is tracked regularly through metrics or milestones. Surveys and self-report scales can be used to assess changes in areas such as confidence or job performance before and after coaching. Clients are encouraged to keep journals to document their experiences and reflections, providing qualitative insights into their development. Regular feedback sessions, where both the coach and client review achievements and challenges, also contribute to measuring progress. By combining these methods, coaches can provide a comprehensive picture of the client's growth and the effectiveness of the coaching process.
